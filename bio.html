BIOGRAPHY

Marcello Carelli, 20 years old, one of the most versatile musicians on the music scene today. Majoring in Studio Music and Jazz at the University of Miami's Frost School of Music, Carelli is a junior Stamps Distinguished Ensemble Scholar, having been chosen out of many applicants to play in a high profile jazz quintet for four years. Along with this group, Carelli has also played in the award-winning Frost Concert Jazz Band and Brian Lynch Artist Enemble He has also gained a variety of skills in performance, arraning, and composition over these four years. Inside and outside of school, Marcello has performed with already played with many esteemed artists including Brian Lynch, Dave Liebman, Ira Sullivan, John Daversa, Andy Fusco, and Walt Weiskopf.

Originally from Bedford Hills, NY, Carelli attended the Manhattan School of Music's Precollege Program with a concentration in both jazz and classical performance. There, he was awarded the D’Addario Scholarship and the MSM Jazz Scholarship. Marcello was a member of the 2017 All-Eastern and All-National Jazz Ensembles. He was recognized as a Young Arts Foundation Merit and Honorable Mention winner in Jazz in 2016 and 2017, as well as an Outstanding Soloist at the Berklee High School Jazz festival.

Marcello has performed at the Umbria and Pescara Jazz Festivals in Italy as well as the Crescent City Jazz Festival in New Orleans. He has also traveled to Surinam, California, and Colorado with the Stamps Jazz Quintet to perform and teach masterclasses. Marcello has been exposed to music since he was very young, thanks to his parents who are both performing artists. He is a product of many fine teachers, including 2011 MacArthur Fellow, Dafnis Prieto, and 2008 Los Angeles Music Awards Nominee, Jack Evans. Marcello’s drumming influences include Art Blakey, Philly Joe Jones, Louis Hayes, Kenny Washington, and Greg Hutchinson.
